Thako Devi Jogendra Bhagat Sewa Trust (TJB Sewa Trust)

A family philanthropic trust founded by Jogendra Bhagat and Thako Devi Jogendra Bhagat, committed to donating their family's assets entirely to social work in Saptari. Now widely regarded as one of the most exemplary trusts of its kind in Nepal, not just Saptari, its institutions span health, education, emergency services and culture across Rajbiraj, Rupani, Shambhunath and Bishnupur.

The convener's role: Sanjog Dev served as the Trust's acting president in 2022, leading it through the Rupani hospital's inauguration, and remains engaged in its overall planning and development — one of the previous works Saptari Next builds upon.

Institutions built or supported by the Trust

  • Madhesh Pradesh Police Hospital, Rupani — 2 bigha of land and an 18,000 sq ft hospital building constructed by the Trust and handed over to Nepal Police in 2072 BS, along with NPR 2.5 lakh cash and equipment; the Trust also built the 8,400 sq ft staff quarters. Now a 15-bed hospital with ambulance service, 24-hour emergency care, X-ray, thyroid and cholesterol/sugar testing, run by 52 staff including 3 consultant doctors — inaugurated in 2022.
  • ThakoDeviJogendraBhagat Lions Club ENT Hospital, Rajbiraj — land donated on Gajendra Narayan Singh Road, Ward 3, for an ENT (nose, ear, throat) hospital built with the Lions Club of Saptari, serving patients who previously had to travel to Dharan, Birgunj, Janakpur or Kathmandu.
  • Rajdevi ThakoDevi Jogendra Bhagat Secondary School, Rajbiraj — NPR 51 lakh spent on physical infrastructure for secondary-level teaching, built in 2044 BS.
  • Nepal APF ThakoDevi Jogendra Bhagat School, Shambhunath — 8 bigha 11 kattha of land handed to the Armed Police Force in 2072 BS to build the school (now an NPR 18-crore+ campus); a further 3 bigha handed over afterwards. Opened in 2079 BS with 162 students across grades 4–6, the school reserves 40% of seats for the general public alongside APF families, and the Trust funds full scholarships for two underprivileged students a year.
  • Nepal Red Cross Society, Saptari Bhawan — Jogendra Bhagat, a former Saptari Red Cross chairman and vice-chairman, contributed NPR 21 lakh in 2053 BS toward the society's first building and has supported it financially since; Saptari Red Cross now runs 16 sub-branches, 2 support committees and about 4,000 active members.
  • Hindi Sahitya Bhawan, Kathauna, Saptari — 3 kattha of land donated on the Shambhunath-7, Kathauna highway for a literature building with a library, meant for gatherings, seminars and study.

Future plans

The Trust has announced plans to convert the Bhagat family's own Rajbiraj residence into a dharmashala once their full estate passes to the Trust, and to run an ambulance service reaching Rupani, Bishnupur and Shambhunath municipalities.

Setubandh — सेतुबन्ध

Bridging hearts and minds together · a civic society movement

Setubandh is a civic society movement that connects Nepal's intellectuals and leading minds in shaping the destiny of the nation together. As part of the campaign, convener Sanjog Dev travelled to about 57 districts of Nepal alongside his mentor Anand Aditya, building the nationwide network of relationships and civic method that Saptari Next now applies at district scale. Setubandh is a partner organisation of Saptari Next.
