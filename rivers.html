Rivers & River Corridors

Rivers are life. The rivers of Saptari are like the veins of the human body — numerous, branching, carrying life to every corner of the district. From the mighty Sapta Koshi on the east to the Balan on the west, with the Khando, Mahuli, Jita, Ghordah and many seasonal streams between them, water has always written Saptari's story: its fertility, its floods, and its future.

Saptari and its waters — the Sapta Koshi east, Balan west, Chure streams between. Detailed river-corridor maps are being prepared for the Library.

The corridor idea

River corridors — continuous belts of embankment, greenery, walkways, ghats and managed wetlands along each river — can turn Saptari's flood liabilities into public assets: recreation, fisheries, groundwater recharge, tourism and land value. The campaign documents each river's corridor potential and takes stakeholders to the water's edge to see it.

The lakes of Saptari

Beyond its rivers, Saptari holds several lakes and wetlands. The convener has been quietly championing them — taking stakeholders, officials and well-wishers to the lakes so the district's water heritage earns the attention of the concerned authorities. A proposed Khado Lake and eco-tourism destination study is in the campaign archive (see the Library).

Language, culture, food & land

Water shapes culture too. Through the Maithili Wikimedians group, the convener has advocated for Saptari's language, culture, food and land resources in colleges across the region, giving talks that connect students to their heritage.
