VILLAGE MODULE 01

Boria बोरिया

The convener's own village and the template for One Village at a Time. The former Boria VDC, once nine wards, now lives in two local governments: Rajbiraj Municipality Ward 16 and Bishnupur Rural Municipality Ward 5. Understanding this one village deeply, its land, water, people, school, shrines and songs, is how the campaign learns to understand every village.

The Five-Year Framework (VDM Ward 16, 2026–2030)

The module follows the Comprehensive Village Development Model prepared for Ward 16, spanning governance, economic and social pillars, monitored through community scorecards and aligned to the SDGs.

Year 1 — 2026

Baseline surveys and GIS mapping begin alongside governance training, with urgent health post upgrades and school repairs and a pilot vocational training and microfinance programme.

Year 2 — 2027

Roads and drainage works follow, along with scaled-up health services and school development, agricultural modernisation and irrigation, and waste management and solar lighting.

Year 3 — 2028

Social inclusion and GESI campaigns take priority, alongside an entrepreneurship hub and incubation support, organic farming, and community tourism assets and cultural events.

Year 4 — 2029

Services transition to local institutions as monitoring and grievance systems are strengthened and a climate resilience assessment is carried out.

Year 5 — 2030

Handover to community and ward bodies is completed, learning is documented through an After-Action Review, the next five-year plan is drafted, and a local development week exposition marks the close.

Ghordah Sangam Ghat, Majhau — Lake & Hydro Potential

Where the Ghordah meets its confluence at Sangam Ghat, Majhau, the land holds two sleeping possibilities: a lake, retaining the monsoon waters that today rush past as flood, and micro-hydro potential from the regulated flow. A lake here would mean groundwater recharge, fisheries, a public waterfront for Boria and Rajbiraj, and a defence against both flood and drought, while the drop and flow could light the villages around it.

The convener has been walking this ground for years, documenting the site, bringing stakeholders to the ghat, and making the case to authorities; his field record is grouped under Resources below.

Saptari's wider water story, every river, corridor and lake, is on the Rivers & River Corridors page.

Open Source Case Study

An academic case study of Boria itself, part of the village module's evidence base:

Causes and Consequences of In-Migration: A Case Study of Boriya VDC, Saptari

Roshan Kumar Deo · M.A. Sociology thesis, Tribhuvan University (Prithvi Narayan Campus, Pokhara), 2015

A sociology dissertation studying in-migration into Boriya VDC (wards 1, 2 and 5) between 2063–2067 BS, covering household demographics, ethnicity, religion, mother tongue, literacy and income among 52 sampled in-migrant households totalling 265 people; an independent, citable academic record of the village predating the campaign.

Village Timeline

Boria's own history, and how it's been documented since — assembled from records already on this site.

WhenWhat
Pre-2017Boria exists as its own Village Development Committee (VDC) of nine wards.
2017Nepal's federal restructuring dissolves the country's VDCs; Boria's nine wards are absorbed into two new local governments — Rajbiraj Municipality Ward 16 and Bishnupur Rural Municipality Ward 5.
2015Boriya VDC is the subject of an independent academic record predating the campaign: Roshan Kumar Deo's M.A. sociology thesis on in-migration, Tribhuvan University (see Open Source Case Study above).
18 Feb 2023Saptari Next launches district-wide, convened by Sanjog Dev — a Boria native.
2026Boria becomes Module 01 of "One Village at a Time," the template for how the campaign documents every village; Narendra Memorial Janta Secondary School gets its own dedicated registry, followed by its Alumni Network & Association plan and digital Alumni Registry; the Village Diaspora registry opens, beginning with Bipin Deo's profile.
2026–2030The Comprehensive Village Development Model for Ward 16 runs its five-year course (see the framework above).
