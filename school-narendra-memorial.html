1. School Overview

Narendra Memorial Janta Secondary School, also recorded as Narendra Memorial Secondary School, Narendra Memorial Public Secondary School, or नरेन्द्र मेमोरियल जनता मावि, is a public secondary school in Boria (formerly Boria-4, now within Rajbiraj Municipality Ward 16), Saptari. Established in 2036 BS (around 1979–80 AD), it teaches both the general academic stream and a technical and vocational stream, and has been permitted to run Electrical Engineering as a vocational subject; students come mainly from the Yadav, Mandal, Chaudhary and Mehta communities of the surrounding wards.

2. School Development Plan — One Year

A practical, discussable roadmap, not yet approved by the school or SMC, offered as a starting draft for consultation.

Infrastructure

The plan begins with the physical plant: repairing the existing buildings and improving classrooms, adding a proper library along with science and computer laboratories, and introducing smart classrooms where feasible. Reliable drinking water and toilets come next, alongside sports facilities, a playground and a school garden, with a boundary wall, stable electricity and internet connectivity tying the campus together.

Academic Improvement

On the academic side, the priority is building teacher capacity and monitoring attendance more consistently, so that learning outcomes can be tracked honestly. Digital education and reading programmes would sit alongside closer attention to examination performance and expanded scholarship support.

Administration

Administratively, the school would benefit from digitised records and clearer financial transparency, paired with stronger parent engagement, more accountable school governance and deeper community participation in decisions.

Student Development

Beyond the classroom, the plan looks to sports, cultural programmes and debate, science exhibitions and environment clubs, and leadership training and career counselling, so students leave with more than exam results.

The school already runs several home-grown incentive schemes worth building on; see Scholarships below, including teacher-of-the-quarter awards and discipline and attendance prizes documented in 2017.

5. Infrastructure Projects

India-funded school block (2007– )

The foundation stone was laid in 2007 by then Indian Ambassador to Nepal, Shivashankar Mukherjee, with Government of India financing, as part of a wider package of 18 India-financed projects across Saptari; the contractor was Danfe Construction Company of Kathmandu. As of a May 2016 report the block was still without window panes, door panels, wiring or paint, nine years after the foundation stone and years past its original deadline. The Indian Embassy's later inauguration of a facility at the school (see Resources) suggests the project was eventually completed, though the exact completion date is not yet confirmed for this registry.

11. Exemplary Individuals

People whose contribution to the school's development is worth recognising; draft profiles, to be expanded as research progresses.

Khila Nath Gautam

Head teacher, Narendra Memorial Janta Secondary School (2015–17 record)

From Okhaldhunga, he became head teacher on 2072 Bhadau 15 BS (roughly August 2015) after his predecessor, Bhabendra Prasad Yadav, retired on reaching the age limit. Under his leadership the school's students placed first in district-level essay and elocution competitions, a first for a community school in a field usually led by Rajbiraj's institutional schools, as documented in a February 2017 feature on the school's turnaround.
